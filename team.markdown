Naila Butt (she/her) is a Digital Media Specialist and tutor at CUNY City Tech Writing Center. She holds a BS in Professional and Technical Writing and is currently pursuing an M.A. in Digital Humanities at the CUNY Graduate Center, where she explores her research interests in play theory, game studies, and pedagogy. 

She is the project manager of the Pretty Terrifying Project (working name), which she founded to collect data on recurring feminist themes and structures within the horror video game genre. She builds on feminist and horror/game studies frameworks to examine video games, which are often underexamined as cultural and media artifacts. Her methods involve web-scraping data from Wikipedia, Category: Horror video games, and conducting both distant and close reads to collect and categorize the data. In collaboration with her team, Naila aims to refine the dataset and create visualizations that help translate and interpret its complex findings, opening new avenues of study and data for feminist, games, and media scholarship.

Michael Nieves has been in the education field for approximately a decade now. He got his undergrad degree in graphic design and then worked in design, including marketing, UI design and print design. While this was a great experience, he eventually decided that selling and promotion things wasn’t his calling. Instead, through a bit of networking and some luck, he found a position working on accessibility throughout CUNY. Using his graphic design skills to have an impact on those areas that needed a little more love was the spark that interested him. Eventually, as he continued to work his way around CUNY, collaborating with both faculty and students, he stumbled across instructional design. Here he is able to can focus all the skillsets he's acquired over the years and help make education a little better for CUNY's students.

Within this project, Michael is working primarily on the visual design, helping to figure out how to best get our message across visually. He seeks to present our project as simply and clear as possible. Then, should time permit, maybe he can try to push the boundary a bit and put in a little bit of chaos for fun.

Truly Johnson got an undergrad degree in computer science at Hunter College, and currently works as a software engineer. They're still in the process of figuring out their exact research interests, but they're generally curious about 1. the particular mainstream technology and media culture of the present moment and how people deconstruct that as they create art and community on the margins, and 2. how people similarly chafed against dominant views in the past, especially through non-standard expressions of identity and queerness, and how that might inspire and inform people now.

Their responsibilities on this project are mainly focused on the technical aspects of the work. They put together the website you're looking at right now! In addition, they will assist with research and writing documentation and supplementary materials. Truly is excited to both apply skills they have experience in and learn and experiment with new skills through the course of this project.
